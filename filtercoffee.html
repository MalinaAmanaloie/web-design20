Filter Coffee

Manual filter coffee is a complex process, and its preparation is a ritual worthy of a chemist. Manual filtering is a method of preparation that connects you with the origin of coffee beans.

For this method of preparation we recommend using a medium light roasted coffee, or even light. For extra acidity you can try a coffee that comes from Africa.

Recipe

- Coffee: 18g (60g/ 1l water)
- Grinding degree: medium (6)
- Water: 240 g
- Water temperature: 80-90°C
- Pre-infusion: 4 x 30 seconds x 50g + 40g water
- Total preparation time: 2:30 minutes 

Step by step recipe

- Place the paper filter in the dripper and moisten it with hot water, so you will get rid of the cellulose taste of the paper filter.
- Grind and distribute the coffee in the dripper
- Pre-infuse with a very thin stream of water, using 50 g for 30 seconds.
- After 1 minute, pour 50 g of water, forming a spiral shape, from center to outside. The water stream must be so thin that would fall 90 degrees from the beak of the vessel.
- Pour the rest of the water, in one turn of 100 g, so as to keep the coffee beans submerged.
- Pour the extraction into a mug and enjoy it!
